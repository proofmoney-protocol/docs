# ProofMoney Risk Disclosure v1.0

**Version:** v1.0  
**Project:** ProofMoney  
**Initial Author:** Vingen Motoki  
**Positioning:** An open protocol for verifiable money integrity  
**Core Statement:** If money cannot be verified, it is only a promise.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Risk Disclosure explains important risks related to ProofMoney, PRM, ProofMoney software, test environments, documentation, public infrastructure, and any future protocol deployment.

ProofMoney is experimental.

Nothing in ProofMoney documentation, software, websites, repositories, community channels, or public communications should be interpreted as investment advice, financial advice, legal advice, tax advice, or a promise of future value.

---

## 2. No Investment Offer

ProofMoney does not offer, sell, or solicit the purchase of PRM.

ProofMoney documentation is provided for protocol design, technical discussion, and open-source development purposes.

Nothing in ProofMoney materials should be interpreted as an offer to buy, sell, subscribe to, or invest in PRM.

---

## 3. No Profit Expectation

ProofMoney does not promise profit.

ProofMoney does not promise:

- price increase;
- guaranteed returns;
- yield;
- income;
- dividends;
- buybacks;
- redemption;
- market support;
- floor price;
- exchange listing;
- liquidity;
- future appreciation.

PRM may never have any market value.

---

## 4. Experimental Protocol Risk

ProofMoney is an experimental open protocol design.

The protocol may fail due to:

- flawed assumptions;
- incomplete design;
- implementation errors;
- security vulnerabilities;
- insufficient network participation;
- poor user experience;
- economic model failure;
- governance failure;
- infrastructure failure;
- lack of adoption;
- regulatory restrictions;
- community fragmentation;
- operational mistakes.

---

## 5. Software Risk

ProofMoney software may contain bugs.

Software risks include:

- consensus bugs;
- ledger state errors;
- supply calculation errors;
- wallet bugs;
- transaction validation bugs;
- signature verification bugs;
- private key handling errors;
- networking failures;
- database corruption;
- dependency vulnerabilities.

Open-source software can still contain critical vulnerabilities.

---

## 6. Private Key and Ownership Risk

Control over assets depends on control over keys or authorization structures.

If a user loses access to private keys, recovery information, signing devices, or required multi-signature participants, the user may permanently lose access.

ProofMoney cannot guarantee recovery of lost keys or lost access.

---

## 7. Transaction Risk

Transactions may be irreversible.

Users may lose assets if they send to the wrong address, sign a malicious transaction, interact with fake software, misunderstand fees, or use compromised devices.

Users are responsible for verifying transaction details before signing.

---

## 8. Test Environment Risk

ProofMoney test environments are for testing only.

Test units:

- have no monetary value;
- are not PRM;
- are not redeemable for PRM;
- do not represent ownership rights;
- do not create future allocation rights;
- should not be bought or sold;
- may be reset, deleted, or invalidated at any time.

Testing is not a financial opportunity.

---

## 9. Regulatory Risk

Regulatory treatment of digital assets, open protocols, software, wallets, public networks, and related services varies by jurisdiction and may change over time.

ProofMoney does not guarantee that PRM or any ProofMoney-related activity will be legally permitted in any jurisdiction.

Users and contributors are responsible for understanding applicable laws in their own jurisdictions.

---

## 10. Third-Party Risk

Third parties may build tools, services, communities, markets, wallets, analytics products, or infrastructure around ProofMoney.

ProofMoney cannot guarantee the safety, accuracy, legality, or reliability of third-party services.

Users should independently verify any third-party service before use.

---

## 11. Summary of Key Risks

ProofMoney may fail.

PRM may never have value.

PRM may have no liquidity.

ProofMoney software may contain bugs.

Private keys may be lost or stolen.

Transactions may be irreversible.

Test units have no value.

Third-party services may be fraudulent or unsafe.

Regulatory treatment is uncertain.

Users may lose time, money, data, or access.

Participation is at the user’s own risk.

---

## 12. Final Statement

ProofMoney is designed around verification, not financial promises.

Verification does not eliminate risk.

If money cannot be verified, it is only a promise.

If risk cannot be understood, participation should be avoided.
