# ProofMoney Project Principles v1.0

**Version:** v1.0  
**Project:** ProofMoney  
**Initial Author:** Vingen Motoki  
**Positioning:** An open protocol for verifiable money integrity  
**Core Statement:** If money cannot be verified, it is only a promise.

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the highest-level principles of ProofMoney.

ProofMoney does not exist to become a speculative narrative, a fundraising vehicle, or a private allocation system.

ProofMoney begins with a single question:

> **How can money integrity be verified by anyone?**

---

## 2. Core Definition

ProofMoney is an open protocol initiative for **verifiable money integrity**.

It is designed to allow independent verification of:

- issuance;
- supply;
- ownership;
- flow;
- rules;
- public protocol funding;
- starting state integrity.

---

## 3. What ProofMoney Is Not

ProofMoney is not:

- a private token sale;
- an investment product;
- a yield program;
- a trading community;
- a fundraising campaign;
- a custodial exchange;
- a promise of future liquidity;
- a shortcut to trust.

ProofMoney should never be described as a financial opportunity.

---

## 4. The Five Proofs

ProofMoney is built around five core proofs.

### Proof of Issuance

Every unit must have a verifiable origin.

### Proof of Supply

Supply should be computed, not claimed.

### Proof of Ownership

A balance is not ownership. Control is ownership.

### Proof of Flow

Flow must be verifiable. Privacy must be respected.

### Proof of Rule

A rule is real only when it is verifiably enforced.

---

## 5. Zero-Privilege Principle

ProofMoney must begin without monetary privilege:

- no initial founder allocation;
- no private sale;
- no insider round;
- no hidden address;
- no guaranteed yield;
- no test-unit conversion promise;
- no unilateral rule privilege.

---

## 6. Verification-First Principle

Verification is not an advanced feature.

Verification is the product.

Every critical monetary claim should have a verification path.

---

## 7. Language Boundaries

ProofMoney communication must not use:

- guaranteed returns;
- price predictions;
- private sale language;
- early wealth language;
- exchange listing promises;
- yield promises;
- airdrop bait;
- buy pressure.

ProofMoney communication should focus on:

- protocol integrity;
- proof models;
- open-source review;
- risk disclosure;
- user control;
- public verification.

---

## 8. Author Principle

Initial documents may be authored by Vingen Motoki.

However:

> **The protocol is intended to outgrow its initial author.**

ProofMoney should not depend on founder personality, celebrity, or authority.

It should depend on verification.

ProofMoney does not offer, sell, or solicit the purchase of PRM. PRM does not guarantee price, liquidity, yield, profit, or exchange access. ProofMoney is experimental and may fail. Test units, if any, have no monetary value.
