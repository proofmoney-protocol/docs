# ProofMoney Manifesto v1.0

**Version:** v1.0  
**Project:** ProofMoney  
**Initial Author:** Vingen Motoki  
**Positioning:** An open protocol for verifiable money integrity  
**Core Statement:** If money cannot be verified, it is only a promise.

---

## 1. Money Runs on Promises

Modern money is built on promises.

Banks promise custody.  
Platforms promise balances.  
Exchanges promise reserves.  
Issuers promise rules.  
Applications promise that what users see on screen is real.

But for most people, these promises are difficult to verify.

A balance can be displayed without proof.  
A reserve can be claimed without proof.  
A supply number can be published without proof.  
A rule can be announced without proof.  
An asset can appear to be owned without real control.

When money cannot be verified, users are forced to trust systems they cannot inspect.

ProofMoney begins with a simple belief:

> **If money cannot be verified, it is only a promise.**

---

## 2. From Promises to Proofs

ProofMoney is not designed to create another speculative asset.

ProofMoney is designed to address a deeper problem:

> **How can money integrity be verified by anyone, without relying on institutional promises?**

Money integrity means more than a visible balance.

It means the ability to verify where money comes from, how much exists, who controls it, how it moves, and whether its rules have changed.

ProofMoney exists to turn these questions into verifiable proofs.

Not trust.  
Not reputation.  
Not marketing.  
Not screenshots.  
Not platform statements.

Proof.

---

## 3. The Five Proofs

ProofMoney is built around five core proofs.

### Proof of Issuance

Every unit of money must have a verifiable origin.

Principle:

> **Every unit must have a source that can be proven.**

### Proof of Supply

The total supply must not be a statement.

It must be a calculation anyone can verify.

Principle:

> **Supply should be computed, not claimed.**

### Proof of Ownership

A displayed balance is not the same as ownership.

Principle:

> **A balance is not ownership. Control is ownership.**

### Proof of Flow

Money must move through verifiable state transitions.

Principle:

> **Flow must be verifiable. Privacy must be respected.**

### Proof of Rule

Rules must not live only in documents or announcements.

Principle:

> **A rule is real only when it is verifiably enforced.**

---

## 4. Zero-Privilege Launch

ProofMoney must begin without monetary privilege.

There is no initial allocation to founders.  
There is no private sale.  
There is no insider round.  
There is no hidden address.  
There is no guaranteed yield.  
There is no privileged rule change.

The starting point must be verifiable.

ProofMoney does not ask users to believe that the launch was fair.

It must allow them to verify it.

---

## 5. Closing Statement

ProofMoney does not ask the world for blind trust.

It offers a path to verification.

It does not replace one promise with another.

It replaces promises with proofs.

If money cannot be verified, it is only a promise.

**ProofMoney turns money from a promise into proof.**
